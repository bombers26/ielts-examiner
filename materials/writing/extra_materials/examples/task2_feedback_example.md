## Four-criterion diagnosis

- Task Response: Position is partly clear at the start, but the conclusion weakens it by saying both are important without answering priority clearly.
- Coherence & Cohesion: Basic paragraph logic is present, but ideas are underdeveloped.
- Lexical Resource: Clear but repetitive: public transport, roads, important.
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: Mostly accurate simple clauses; limited complex structures.

## Targeted revision plan

1. State a stable position: public transport should receive priority, while roads still need maintenance.
2. Develop two reasons: capacity/environment; social access/affordability.
3. Use one concrete example but explain the mechanism.

## Improved sample paragraph

Governments should prioritise public transport because it moves far more people within limited urban space. A single metro line or bus corridor can reduce the number of private cars entering crowded districts, which lowers congestion and pollution more effectively than simply widening roads. Roads still require maintenance, especially for emergency services and freight, but new spending should first address the transport mode that benefits the largest number of residents.
